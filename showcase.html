NumberJump

An interactive floor-movement game where kids jump to numbered zones — guided by audio prompts and confirmed by a camera overhead.

Python · Pygame OpenCV Raspberry Pi Ages 3–15 Finnish & English Open source

How it works

Five components, zero complexity to operate.

🗂️

Floor mat

Nine numbered paper squares arranged 3 × 3 on the ground, each ~50 cm.

🧦

Bright sock

The player wears a lime-green (or orange) sock. The camera tracks it by color.

📷

Overhead camera

A RaspiCam or USB webcam looks straight down and sees where the sock lands.

🔊

Audio prompt

"Jump to number 7!" — the speaker tells the player what to do next.

✅

Auto-confirm

Three consecutive frames on the right zone triggers "Correct!" — no button press needed.

Game modes

Difficulty scales with age — same hardware, different rules.

Jump

Hyppää / Jump

A single number is called out. The player runs and jumps onto that square before time runs out.

"Jump to number 7!"
Math

Lasku / Math

An addition or subtraction problem is read aloud. The player jumps to the zone showing the answer.

"What is 3 + 4?" → jump to 7
Sequence

Järjestys / Sequence

Three numbers are called in order. The player must visit each zone in the exact sequence given.

"Jump in order: 2 → 5 → 8"

Age tiers

One game, three skill levels — select at startup.

Tier Ages Zones Timer Modes available
Pikku 3–5 1–3 9 s Jump only
Juniori 6–10 1–9 6 s Jump · Addition · Sequence
Haaste 11–15 1–9 3 s Jump · Addition · Subtraction · Sequence

Tech stack

All open-source, runs on a €40 Raspberry Pi.

🐍

Python 3

Game loop, state machine, audio scheduling, and CLI — all in plain Python.

🎮

Pygame

Window, rendering, font drawing, keyboard events, and display loop at 30 fps.

👁️

OpenCV

Color-based HSV blob detection, perspective transform (homography) from calibration corners, centroid tracking.

📷

Picamera2 / V4L2

Auto-detects Raspberry Pi CSI camera or falls back to any USB/built-in webcam via OpenCV VideoCapture.

🔢

NumPy

Camera frame arrays and the 3×3 perspective transform matrix used to map pixel coordinates to mat zones.

🔊

WAV audio clips

Pre-recorded .wav files played via Pygame mixer. Supports Finnish and English; sfx are language-neutral.

Hardware

Everything you need — most is already in a school or home.

Raspberry Pi 3 (or any PC / laptop)
RaspiCam (CSI) or USB webcam
Wired 3.5 mm speaker
9 numbered paper squares (3 × 3 grid)
Lime-green or orange sock
HDMI monitor (optional — runs headless for audio)
